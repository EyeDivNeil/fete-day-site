Fete Day is a creative production company working across the UK with a focus on projects in Oxford and Lincolnshire. Led by Ameneh Enayat and Rosemary Richards, Fete Day works with arts producer and heritage professionals to create high quality arts and heritage events, often working with the participation of communities.

Rosemary Richards

Rosemary is a creative producer and cultural consultant. Current projects include Arts Producer for Birmingham Cathedral's 300th anniversary celebrations, Creative Director/Producer for Steep Hill Stories, a BBC event/media commission for Lincoln's Great Magna Carta weekend, video producer for Wiley Publishing's academic international online projects, and production development on a planned BBC co-production World War One event for Lincoln in 2016. Rosemary formed Rosa Productions in 2011 after working for 7 years for the BBC as Editor, Video Nation and Editor of Interactive and Outreach. Previously her background was in TV, Festivals, Media and Communications. She is also a film-maker with awards for TV documentary and programme making and the BBC Lipdub Lincoln in 2013 won national awards.

Ameneh Enayat

Ameneh is a Director of Rosa Productions and a producer of media, events, heritage projects and engagement projects. She has worked as a TV and communities project producer for the BBC and for many other production and community organisations. Ameneh has particular expertise as a producer for large scale community media and festival inclusion projects. Current projects include production on Alice's Day for the Story Museum, Oxford.

Associates

Jeremy James

Jeremy is Artistic Director of Greenwich and Lewisham Young People's Theatre and is an outdoor events producer and director. He is also co-producer of the Greenwich Children's Festival. Jeremy has worked with Rosa Productions on Gravity Fields Festival and our BBC event collaborations.
